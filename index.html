I’ve spent nearly my whole life writing stories. As a kid, I was always making things with paper and by first grade had determined I wanted to be an artist for our 'what do I want to be when I grow up' book.

It wasn’t until middle school that I had the epiphany that game design would be a good fit. I enjoyed computer science class because a new avenue of making things opened up. I was never good with presentations but I had to make a game and present it to one of my classes and it was the first presentation I actually felt confident doing. That coalesced into me trying out a technology elective in junior high, and then computer science classes the entirety of my high school in Cincinnati, Ohio, where I attended k-12. I now have a minor in computer science and am completing my game design major this semester at Miami University.

My time there allows me to grow both programming wise as well as improving project management skills, teamwork, communication, organization, and public speaking. Additionally, I currently work as a student assistant in our school’s disability services center. They’ve been an amazing resource for me and I’m incredibly appreciative of the position as it teaches me more about accessibility, work environments, and support.

All of these different experiences, as well as my first job as a teaching assistant in a pre-school classroom at my synagogue, have taught me that game design, play, learning, and accessibility can all be intertwined. Now I want to make games that can achieve that through aesthetics, enjoyment, disability representation, accessibility, and education.
