Early on in my computer science minor I took CSE 251, which was Intro to Game Programming. This got me familiar with Unity engine as well as programming in C#. My skills have improved a lot since this rendition of the game, and I'm now working on another iteration in an independent study for my final computer science course before graduating. I'm going to transition the game into Unreal engine and work with C++ and/or blueprints.

For my first rendition of Finish Lime, I developed artwork, animation systems, particles, scoring, saving, exporting a build to be played in browser, shooting, power-ups, and enemies. You play as a fruit-themed racer trying to last as long as they can on the track. By clicking the up arrow, you can destroy any rocks that get in your way, or avoid them by using the arrow keys. Different lemons fall toward you, allowing you to collect them and giving different boosting effects. You have three lives and to replay once they run out simply left-click.

The first iteration was great for learning how to implement systems and for seeing everything come together in one of the first games I made not on a team. I hope to improve upon everything as I continue working on it. A major change is that I want a lot of the gameplay to be in 3D, which means I will need to work on improving my skills in Maya and/or Blender. However, some of the UI screens will likely be 2D in order to showcase the character art. All that really comes across in the current version of the game is that there are a lot of lemons. I want to be clearer with the aesthetics going forward. I did a lot of character art for Luelle Amon, who you're playing as, but you're never introduced to her name or anything else about her. I'm also really looking forward into introducing more accessibility features and disability representation.

To the left and below, you can view different drawing timelapses of and current artwork and models, like what I made in IMS 319, which was Foundations in Digital 3-D Modeling and Animation. More will be coming soon as I continue to further develop this project over the course of Spring 2026.
